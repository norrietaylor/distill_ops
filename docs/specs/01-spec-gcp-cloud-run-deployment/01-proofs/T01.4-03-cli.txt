The iam.serviceAccountUser binding is SA-scoped (applied directly to the
distillery-run SA resource, NOT at project level). This is the security
gate that lets distillery-deployer impersonate distillery-run during CI
deploy without broader project-level impersonation permission.

--- RECORDED GCLOUD INVOCATION (per run) ---
gcloud iam service-accounts add-iam-policy-binding \
  distillery-run@test-proj.iam.gserviceaccount.com \
  --project=test-proj \
  --member=serviceAccount:distillery-deployer@test-proj.iam.gserviceaccount.com \
  --role=roles/iam.serviceAccountUser

Across 2 runs: 2 invocations; idempotent (no policy drift).

Why SA-scoped and not project-scoped: per spec Security Considerations,
we minimize blast radius. Granting iam.serviceAccountUser at project
scope would let the deployer impersonate any SA in the project; scoping
it to the distillery-run SA resource restricts impersonation to that
single identity.
